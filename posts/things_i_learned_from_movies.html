Things I learned from Movies

by Jeremy Osborne, 2011.08.14 21:12:44

I have pretty simple hobbies, and one of them is movie watching. I love movies, all kinds: good ones, bad ones, super awesome B-Movies with high budgets for special effects and no budgets for acting, the inverse of the just referenced type of movie. Most movies really have nothing to offer other than a few hours of hedonistic pleasure and the pure joy of being entertained. Every now and then, there's something that I learn from a movie that nothing else in life—not school, not my parents, not my wife, not my friends—taught me. Below are my favorites:

Ferris Bueller's Day Off: Sticking things through to the end can have an unexpected reward.

Ghostbusters: On the spot wordplay and adlib is funnier than the best planned jokes.

Starship Troopers: Coed showers are awesome!

Harold & Kumar Go to White Castle: No, you can't ride a cheetah. And girls fart.

The Bad News Bears: Losing sucks, but when I lose, sometimes I should be a man and take my loss.

The Last Samurai: Losing sucks, but when I lose, sometimes I should be a man and get my ass right back up and stand up for myself.
